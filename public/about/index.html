Nice to meet you!#

I’m Rachel - a fourth-year PhD Candidate in Neuroscience at UNC Chapel Hill with a passion for science writing, communications, community engagement, and mentorship. I love meeting and working with new people and make an effort to bring creativity, fun, and inclusion into everything I do. Let’s connect!

Background#

I grew up in Coweta, Oklahoma, and earned a B.S. in Psychology and a minor in Criminology from the University of Oklahoma in 2019. I then worked for two years as a bioinformatician at the Univerity of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center Core Facilities, before coming to UNC in the Fall of 2021 to pursue my PhD in Neuroscience with a certificate in bioinformatics. In 2022, I began exploring the world of science writing and communications and found my passion for communicating science in an engaging and accessible way.

Research Focus#

Currently, I study how social and environmental factors, like access to quality medical care and stress, affect genetic risk for bipolar disorder. I use publically available data to explore this question and focus on the ethical, legal, and social implications of both my research findings and this field of research itself.
